OVERVIEW

If exploring the Rocky Mountains has been high on your bucket list, then the Western Canada - Rocky Mountaineer trip is just what you need. This 10-day trip is curated for families who want to explore the great outdoors together. You arrive in Vancouver and later, a ferry ride will take you to Victoria and after which, there is a rail journey planned from Vancouver to Kamloops. From there on, this Rocky Mountain adventure continues by train and you move to Jasper where after sightseeing you travel by road to Lake Louise and the resort town of Banff.

  • On Day 1, on arrival in Vancouver you visit FlyOver Canada for an interesting 4D Show.
  • On Day 2, you go on the Grouse Mountain and Capilano Suspension Bridge Tour. And while on the tour you engage in a couple interesting activities.
  • On Day 3, you take the BC Ferries ride to Swartz Bay in Victoria and explore Victoria.
  • On Day 4, you travel by train from Vancouver to Kamloops journeying through the scenic Fraser Canyon, and the Thompson River.
  • On Day 5, you continue on your Rocky Mountain adventure by train from Kamloops to Jasper visiting the highest peak - Mount Robson, the Pyramid Falls and the Jasper National Park.
  • On Day 6, you go sightseeing in and around Jasper.
  • On Day 7, you head to Lake Louise via some very scenic roadways and go sightseeing in Lake Louise.
  • On Day 8, you spend in leisure and explore some interesting things to do in Lake Louise.
  • On Day 9, you explore the beautiful resort town of Banff and go sightseeing.
  • On Day 10, your trip comes to a close and you are taken to the Calgary Airport for your onward journey home.

ITINERARY

DAY 1

Vancouver

Arrive in Vancouver International Airport, a vehicle from the car rental agency will pick you up, and you proceed to the hotel. In the evening, visit the FlyOver Canada for a 4D show (the tickets are included in the package). Here you get to experience flight ride technology. Chairlift-style seats are elevated before a large spherical screen creating an illusion of flying over the landscapes.

You stay overnight in a Vancouver hotel.

DAY 2

Vancouver

After breakfast, proceed on the Grouse Mountain and Capilano Suspension Bridge Tour. After you cross the Lions Gate Bridge, look-out for the British Property's exclusive homes. Enroute you also visit the Capilano Salmon Hatchery and you continue to the Capilano Suspension bridge. The Capilano experience is a heart-stopping Cliffwalk and Treetops Adventure. Later you proceed to Grouse Mountain, and take the famous Skyride to the mountaintop. Once atop the Grouse Mountain, you stroll around engaging in activities like Guided bird watching tours, Theatre in the Sky "Born to Fly", seasonal logging shows, Peak Chairlift, Lumberjack Show, the Eye of the Wind and much more.

You stay overnight in a Vancouver hotel.

DAY 3

Vancouver

After breakfast, you proceed on a full day tour of Victoria City with a 90 Minute Ferry Ride on BC Ferries through the Gulf Islands. You sail across the Strait of Georgia, to reach Swartz Bay. You disembark and drive to the Butchart Gardens. One of Victoria’s most celebrated sites and rated amongst the most beautiful gardens in the world. Later, make your way to Victoria’s Inner Harbour, and check out the maritime heritage and colonial charm. You also get to explore the world-famous Fairmont Empress Hotel, British Import Ships, and Parliament Buildings here.

You return to Vancouver by the ferry and overnight in a hotel in Vancouver.

DAY 4

Vancouver to Kamloops

Early morning, check-out and transfer to the station to travel on-board the Rocky Mountaineer Rail and enjoy world-class hospitality, from Vancouver to Kamloops, passing through the rushing waters of Hell’s Gate in the Fraser Canyon, the steep slopes and rock sheds along the Thompson River and enjoy the beautiful journey.

You stay overnight in a Kamloops hotel.

DAY 5

Kamloops to Jasper

Early morning your Rocky Mountaineer Rail adventure continues to the mighty Canadian Rockies and the province of Alberta. The highlights include visiting Mount Robson, the highest peak in the Canadian Rockies, the Pyramid Falls and the climb over the Yellowhead Pass into the Jasper National Park.

On arrival, transfer from the Jasper train station to the hotel.

You stay overnight in a Jasper hotel.

DAY 6

Jasper

After breakfast, you proceed on an Explore Jasper Tour. You can leisurely spend the day to experience the uniquely beautiful landscape of the Jasper National Park where history is set amid some of the most rugged peaks in the Rockies. You explore the Jasper town-site, and visit places like the Athabasca Viewpoint, the Maligne Canyon, and the Maligne Lake. At Maligne Canyon, you stroll along 50 meter walls and learn about its unique underground water system.

Later, enjoy a Maligne Lake Boat Cruise (optional). And we suggest a visit to the Miette Hot Springs, and you should go whitewater rafting, take the Jasper Skytram, and go Sky Gazing and much more.

You stay overnight in a hotel in Jasper.

DAY 7

Jasper to Lake Louise via Columbia Icefields

After breakfast, you check-out and drive to Lake Louise via the Icefields Parkway - Canada’s most scenic mountain roadway, a route to glaciers, lakes, and waterfalls. Arrive at the Columbia Icefields Discovery Center. Here, you take the Ice Explorer Ride which takes you up the Athabasca Glacier. Later, take a coach from the Discovery Center to the Glacier Skywalk, it is a glass-floored observation platform over the Sunwapta Valley, and you get an unobstructed view of the surrounding glaciers. Continue along the Parkway to the Crowfoot Glacier, the Peyto Lake and Bow Lake enroute to Lake Louise.

You stay overnight in a Lake Louise hotel.

DAY 8

Lake Louise to Banff

After breakfast, check-out and keep the luggage in the lobby. You spend the rest of the morning at leisure and you are free to enjoy the charms of the beautiful Lake Louise. Lake Louise is known for its turquoise, glacier-fed lake surrounded by high peaks and a stately chateau overlooks it. Explore multiple optional activities like kayaking, hiking trails winding up to Lake Agnes, visiting the Spa, taking the Lake Louise Gondola and much more.

You later transfer to Banff. On arrival, check-in at the hotel and you spend the rest of the day at leisure to explore the famous Banff Avenue.

You stay overnight in a Banff hotel.

DAY 9

Banff

After breakfast, proceed on an Explore Banff Tour. The resort town of Banff, set amongst the towering mountains of Rundle, Tunnel, Cascade and Norquay. Explore Bow Falls, Tunnel Mountain Drive, Surprise Corner, Hoodoos, Two Jack Viewpoint, and Lake Minnewanka Boat Cruise (optional) for one hour wherein you get to witness the wildlife and vegetation and visit the Devil’s gap at the foothills of the Rockies. A gentle ride on the Banff Gondola takes you to the summit of the Sulphur Mountain for a 360 degree panoramic view of the Bow Valley. You get the afternoon to spend in leisure and you can explore the resort town of Banff.

You stay overnight in a Banff hotel.

DAY 10

Banff to Calgary

After breakfast, your trip comes to a close. You check-out and transfer to the Calgary airport for your connecting flight to your onward destination back home.

WHEN TO GO

Irrespective of Canada’s size, the weather is almost similar across the country from the west to the east. Essentially, winters are very cold in all parts of Canada and summers are warm and pleasant. So that is why the spring-summer months are the best months to visit Canada. Even the fall or autumn months- September-October is good to visit certain places. If talking about visiting the British Columbia area in Canada, there is no specific peak season. But travel anywhere between June to September is ideal. Now if you are thinking of visiting Vancouver, Kamloops, Jasper, Lake Louise, Banff, and Calgary it is best to travel anytime between June and October. During this time, it is moderately warm and dry.
