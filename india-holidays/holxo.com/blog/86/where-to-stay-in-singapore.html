Where to Stay in Singapore

by Travio Travel Specialist

SINGAPORE
WHERE TO STAY

Singapore is one of the fastest growing travel hubs in Southeast Asia. Plush, developed and lined with some of the most stunning skyscrapers in the world, the destination is a favored holiday choice for several tourists. The city mostly welcomes you with awe-inspiring infrastructure and towering buildings, however, inside its nook and corners lies a world that is altogether different. Its uninhibited charm and mysterious whiff makes it a beautiful destination with several options to stay and revel in.

Although believed to be an expensive tourist destination, Singapore offers a wide variety of options when it comes to hotels. From business travelers and families to backpackers and budget travelers, the state boasts accommodations for everyone’s tastes. Find below a detailed list of the preferred areas and the best hotels in Singapore:

Marina Bay

One of the swankiest urban areas in the city nation, Marina Bay is the first choice for most visitors in Singapore. Known to be a luxury hub, the area boasts of ultra-modern architecture and several breathtaking man-made structures. It is lined with commercial spaces, plush hotels and restaurants and a range of high-end boutiques and malls and is easily a great pit stop for your Singapore Holiday.

Hotels in Marina Bay:

  • Sofitel So Singapore: Located in a refurbished heritage building in Singapore’s Central Business District, the SO/Sofitel is one of the finest hotels in Marina Bay. It offers luxe French-styled rooms along with a host of luxury amenities.
  • Mandarin Oriental: Ideal for an ultimate urban retreat, the luxe Mandarin Oriental is a perfect choice for those willing to combine work and leisure. The premium hotel is located centrally and offers fine rooms and a range of in-house restaurants.
  • Marina Bay Sands: Fancy taking a dip in the largest infinity pool in the world? Head over to the super-luxe Marina Bay Sands. Hovering over the bay, the luxury hotel offers 20 dining options and direct access to Singapore’s premium mall and the ArtScience Museum. It is by far one of the best hotels in Singapore city centre.

Things to do in Marina Bay: When it comes to places to visit in Singapore, Marina Bay tops the list for several reasons. It houses popular tourist spots such as The Gardens by the Bay, the ArtScience Museum, the Marina Bay Golf Course and the Marina Bay Sands Casino and is home to various swanky bars and restaurants.

Sentosa Island

Heaven for beach lovers, Sentosa is another popular region to stay in the state. The place alone boasts of several pristine beaches and is referred to as the getaway destination of Singapore. Owing to its proximity to the coasts, the region has naturally developed into a tourist hub with several hotels and resorts lining its periphery.

Hotels in Sentosa:

  • Shangri-La’s Rasa Sentosa Resort: Ideal for a tropical holiday experience, the Shangri-La’s Rasa Sentosa resort is one of the best family hotels in Singapore. It is located 2 km from Siloso Beach and is a luxurious option to stay at.
  • Amara Sanctuary Resort: Offering deluxe rooms with a garden or sea view, the Amara Sanctuary Resort is also a great option to stay at Sentosa. It boasts of several luxe amenities and is ideal for large groups and families.
  • Siloso Beach Resort: An affordable and basic hotel on the northern curve of Sentosa, the Siloso Beach Resort is ideal for backpackers and offers clean and budget-friendly stays.

Things to do in Sentosa: A beach paradise, Sentosa is best known for its laid-back island life. When here, visit its several world-renowned theme parks such as the Universal Studios Singapore (USS), S.E.A Aquarium, and Adventure Cove. Alternatively, spend the evenings frolicking by the island’s beaches or catch the Wings of Time, a theatrical event showcasing colorful lights, lasers, water fountains and 3D effects.

Orchard Road

Those riddled with where to stay in Singapore can also consider parking themselves at the infamous Orchard Road. Boasting of 2.2 km of malls, the region is known to be the shopping capital of Singapore and is ideal for those looking for some retail therapy.

Hotels in Orchard Road:

  • Grand Park Orchard: Located centrally and touted as one of the most fashionable hotels in Singapore, is the Grand Park Orchard. It offers a host of luxury services and has also been voted as the best hotel in Singapore 2016 by Agoda.com
  • Sheraton Towers Singapore: Complete with an outdoor pool, a state-of-the-art spa and fitness centre, the Sheraton Towers Singapore is one of the best five-star accommodations on Orchard Road. It offers stunning views of the city centre along with great deals around the year.
  • Orchard Rendezvous Hotel Singapore: Formerly known as the Orchard Parade Hotel, the Orchard Rendezvous Singapore is a great choice for those willing to explore the nearby shopping arcades. It offers a plethora of fine-dining choices along with a range of other luxury amenities.

Things to do in Orchard Road: Famed for its shopping avenues, Orchard Road is best experienced with some retail therapy. One can also visit the Botanic Gardens here or take a stroll through the Fort Canning Park in the region.

Little India

The Indian influences in Singapore are of little surprise and standing testimony to this is Little India. The hipster neighborhood is filled with boutique shops, hotels, and unique street arts and is one of the best places to stay for those culturally inclined.

Hotels in Little India:

  • PARKROYAL on Kitchener Road: Offering a well-appointed 4-star accommodation with sauna is the PARKROYAL on Kitchener Road. Replete with a pool, an in-house spa, and several other amenities, the PARKROYAL is a fitting choice for luxury travellers visiting Little India.
  • Parc Sovereign Hotel - Albert St: Combining affordability with luxury and offering a fine mix of the two is the Parc Sovereign Hotel - Albert St. The hotel houses 172 premium rooms and is centrally located from top-ranking tourist attractions of Singapore.
  • Village Hotel Bugis: Clean and conveniently located, the Village Hotel Bugis is a trusted choice for budget travellers who do not wish to do away with comfort and hygiene. The hotel also offers a buffet and a halal breakfast.
  • Park Hotel Farrer Park: Including plush air-conditioned rooms, a swimming pool and in-house restaurant, the Park Hotel Farrer Park is a good choice for families as well as solo/business travellers. The hotel features glass exteriors and offers panoramic views of the city skyline.

Things to do in Little India: Staying true to its name, Little India offers a myriad of restaurants that serve a delectable Indian spread. Gorge on Indian delicacies and immerse further in its culture with a visit to the Sri Veeramakaliamman Hindu temple in the region. Other nearby attractions include Sim Lim Square, the City Square Mall, and the Singapore Art Museum.

Chinatown

A far cry from the luxuries of modern-day Singapore, Chinatown is a preferred choice for those willing to submerge in culture and experience the strong Chinese influence in the state. The area is complete with cute little neighborhoods and shop houses and is a mesmerizing sight to look at!

Hotels in Chinatown:

  • Amara Singapore: Contrary to Chinatown’s repute for being anything but plush, the Amara Singapore is the most reputed five-star hotels in Singapore.  It is known for its decadent interiors and its wise host of amenities.
  • Adler Singapore: One of the best value hotels in Singapore, the Adler is a well-located hotel in Chinatown. Although not heavy on the pocket, the Adler is known to offer great deals and in-room facilities to guests.

Things to do in Chinatown: When in Chinatown, it is worth paying its several temples and eclectic sites a visit. Stop by the 4-story pagoda Buddha Tooth Relic temple or wander about the infamous street market in the region. It is also worth indulging in some gastronomy by sampling the Michelin-starred barbecued pork at Hawker Chan and several other similar delights.

Rest assured, where to stay in Singapore would hardly be a problem now!
