BHUTAN

Why Bhutan is ideal for your honeymoon

Nestled deep in the Himalayas is the mountain kingdom of Bhutan. In the backdrop of the panoramic Himalayan Mountains, which offers great scenic views, waterfalls and nice gurgling rivers, the word ‘beautiful’ fits Bhutan to the T. Owing to its sereneness and great natural beauty, Bhutan is a perfect honeymoon destination. For couples who want a quiet, romantic holiday, Bhutan qualifies well. You can discover romance in the many charming locations, valleys, and mountains. And at the end of it all, you will not just fall in love and discover each other, but you will also fall head over heels in love with Bhutan, “the Land of Thunderbolt,” guaranteed.

When to go

The ideal time to visit Bhutan would be March-April and September-November.

What cities to visit

Thimpu, the capital city of Bhutan, Trongsa, Bumthang and Punakha

Ideal duration

A romantic getaway of about 7 days for a Bhutan Honeymoon would be ideal

What to see/ Things to do

Bhutan is referred to by many names. It is known and regarded as the ‘Last Shangri-La’. It is also fondly referred to as the land of the thunder dragon. And internationally, the country is rated very high on the Gross National Happiness quotient. And in this very happy country, there’s so much to see and do. You can discover ancient charming cities, visit rare ancient temples and many palaces and fortresses.

You can visit Thimpu, the charming capital city, check out the peaks of Mount Jhomolhari, and go trekking in Paro Valley that is filled with pine forests and more.

The key attractions in Paro Valley include Kyichu Lhakhang, a very old temple, Taktshang Tiger’s Nest monastery, Drukgyel Dzong, which offers a good view of the valley, Rinpung Dzong, a large fortress, and Bhutan’s National Museum.

Your Bhutan honeymoon getaway should include a visit to the Trongsa village which is a historical location and was home to the Wangchuck kings. There are many forts here and the beautiful mountains in the background complete the picture. Key places to visit here include- The Tower of Trongsa Museum, Chendebji Chorten and Tongsa Dzong.

The largest city of Bhutan is its capital city, Thimpu. It reflects the Bhutanese culture to the best. While here you can go shopping in the weekend markets that are filled with unusual shops, visit popular sightseeing places, museums and more.

The key attractions here include - The Fortress of the Glorious Religion - Tashichho Dzong, the weekend market at Chubachu, Palace of Great Bliss - Dechen Phodrang, Dechencholing Palace, the Palace of Profound Meaning of Secret Mantras - Simtoka Dzong, Memorial Chorten, Buddha Tango Monastery, Cheri Monastery, and the Dordenma Statue.

While on your honeymoon destination, if you want to discover Bhutanese culture, then visit the many sacred temples and forts in Bumthang. You can also enjoy some adventure by going on the Wind Horse Treks, visiting the Burning Lake and more.

Another breathtakingly beautiful valley is the Gangtey valley. While here, trek in the Black Mountain National Park, and visit the Gangtey Monastery.

And yes, there’s Punakha too to visit. It is another important city and the weather is relatively pleasant all through the year. Key attractions here include-Chimi L’hakhang Temple, Khamsum Yulley Namgyal Chorten, Sangchhen Dorji Lhuendrup nunnery, and Punakha Dzong.

MALAYSIA

Why Malaysia makes for an ideal honeymoon destination

For a long time and counting, Malaysia has firmly made a place for itself in the list of favorite international honeymooning destinations. There’s something so magical about this country that it seems to draw couples from far and wide. And there are many reasons why this was, is, and will always be one of the best honeymoon destinations in South-east Asia.

Malaysia is the ultimate haven and a tropical paradise for honeymooners. Couples have so many things to do and see that it satisfies the many cravings and interests of visitors. It is filled with many beautiful beaches, rain-forests, markets, malls, gourmet foods and more. And therefore, if couples want to unwind, and relax, there are beaches and beautiful hotels and resorts. There’s also ample scope for adventure, fun, travel, partying and of course discovering local foods, traditions and culture.

If you are looking for intimacy in warm, beautiful surroundings, and want to spend quality time with perfect weather to keep company, what are you really waiting for? If planning a honeymoon, Malaysia, “Truly Asia” it is!

When to go

Malaysia is a year-round destination. The weather is warm all through the year. But there are spells of rains here and there. The rainy seasons on either side of the coast is between the months of November-mid-February and September-October.

What cities to visit

While in Malaysia visit the capital city of Kuala Lumpur. The other cities include, Malacca City, George Town, Kota Kinabalu, Johor Bahru, Ipoh, Kuching, Petaling Jaya, Shah Alam, and Bintulu.

Ideal duration

7 days to 10 days would be the ideal time for a Malaysia honeymoon. Book our Bali and Malaysia Romance Package here

What to see/ things to do

If a good round of adventure is on your minds, head to the Cameron Highlands and trek and hike through the beautiful natural landscape. You can also visit the islands of Borneo and go trekking through the tropical rain-forests.

For a beachside honeymoon, the Redang Island makes the perfect setting. You can go snorkeling and diving, or just relax by the beach, go kayaking, or go on long romantic walks along the beach and watch the sunset together.

Tanjung Aru is another honeymooner’s paradise, a beach destination in Kota Kinabalu. Take a walk along the shoreline, go deep-sea fishing, go on a romantic sunset cruise, take a glass bottomed boat ride and view the coral reefs, and also explore the local street foods. And for a spot of adventure, you can trek together in the Kinabalu National Park.

Many honeymooning couples head to Batu Ferringhi as there's plenty of local life to explore, it is a beachside place and there's loads of water sports activities here. If you are here, you can unwind and take couples spa service, visit art galleries, lounge, and laze around the beach area, visit the UNESCO World Heritage Site - George Town. Or even check out the Penang Botanical Garden together. You can also go shopping in the night market and explore great local food around.

If you want to discover the history and culture of Malaysia, Malacca city showcases all of this. And on a couples outing, visit the many museums, the remains of the old Portuguese church and fort, visit the zoo and the butterfly and reptile sanctuary, visit the old Dutch Town hall, and shop and eat all you can at Jonkers Street, but this is active only during weekends.

VIETNAM

Why Vietnam is ideal for your honeymoon

When Vietnam is mentioned, at first instance, not everyone relate to it as a honeymoon destination. Yet, over time this land has made a place for itself in the popular, international honeymoon destinations. And so, if you want to honeymoon in a land that is typically unknown and would like to have a honeymoon that is a mixed bag with some bit of adventure, culture, beach and quiet thrown in, think of this Asian land with an undeniable past and history, Vietnam.

There’s something to do for everyone. You can choose to laze around the beach, or go trekking or sightseeing, get a bit of adventure or go exploring the local culture, foods, and traditions. In short, when on a Vietnam honeymoon, just be prepared to be swept off your feet.

So, if you want to have a honeymoon destination that is different, and in a place not much is known of, and you want to explore its mysteries, then plan a honeymoon to Vietnam.

When to go

The best time to visit Vietnam is during spring that is during the months of February to April. And it is also good to travel here during autumn between the months of August to October. During this time, the rainfalls aren’t that high, but occasional spells will be there. The temperature too will be moderate and pleasant during these periods.

Irrespective of all this, Vietnam is a tropical destination and it is considered as a year-round travel destination and you can visit here anytime during the year and not just during the best times mentioned to travel here.

What cities to visit

The cities and places to visit while in Vietnam include Hanoi, Hoi An, Ha Long Bay, Hue, Nha Trang, Mekong Delta, Sapa, Ho Chi Minh City or Saigon.

Ideal duration

If on a honeymoon in Vietnam, 7-10 days would be an ideal duration. Check out our Vietnam package here

What to see/ Things to do

The country is S-shaped and so even if you want to explore the country, you can start at one point and reach the other. It has over 3000km of coastline and that being said, there are many beautiful beaches and plenty of beaches to visit too.

If you want to go sightseeing, there is the beautiful limestone-filled tiny islands in the Halong Bay area that is set against rocky terrain. While here you can also go on a cruise along the bay and enjoy some moments of togetherness.

If adventure is what you seek, then visit the Cu Chi Tunnels. Couples can also trek to the Virgin Caves and Drum caves. You can also go trekking in the beautiful Sapa and Mai Chau regions. Couples can also go together to the Cat Ba Island and while here try a round of kayaking in the deep blue waters.

If you want to understand the Vietnamese culture better, then the Ho Chi Minh Trail Tours will give you a deep understanding of the place and its history. Hanoi’s Old Quarter and the UNESCO nominated towns of Hoi An and Hue have a lot to showcase when it comes to local culture.

And for those who want to shop for local stuff, head to the River Market along the Mekong Delta.

For couples who just want to relax and unwind, then the island of Phu Quoc in the Southern part of Vietnam, and also places like Mui Ne, Nha Trang and Phan Thiet offer nice getaway spots.

Honeymooners can go on a unique rail journey, traveling on the Reunification Express and discover the country and the many tourist places along the way.

As part of unique experiences and adventure, you can go cycling together, or take the memorable tour on a Vespa scooter especially at night in Saigon. And how much ever is said or described, actually doing it, and experiencing it in real in each other’s company is a whole different feeling. And words cannot cover it. Or even express it to the maximum.

So, are you ready for a honeymoon in Vietnam?
